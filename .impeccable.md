## Design Context

### Users
Internal product, engineering, algorithm, and business teammates use this desktop app to demo and validate two core workflows: chat-based main-image editing and PSD/PSB batch editing. Their job is to understand the current capability boundary quickly, compare paths, and run a believable end-to-end demo without production-grade operational complexity.

### Brand Personality
Professional, restrained, clear. The product should feel like a focused internal tool with confidence and technical credibility. It should not feel like a marketing landing page, a heavy enterprise dashboard, or an experimental visual concept.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light mode first. Overall experience should follow ChatGPT's tool/settings-page discipline: clean white surfaces, very light borders, strong spacing rhythm, concise copy, calm hierarchy, and minimal ornament. Chat page can lean closer to ChatGPT's main conversation experience, while the rest of the product should feel like its companion tool surfaces. Existing project color tone and VI direction are considered correct and should be preserved rather than replaced wholesale.

### Design Principles
1. Keep the product quiet: reduce visual noise before adding any decorative treatment.
2. Explain capability through layout, copy, and state, not through heavy cards or dashboard motifs.
3. Preserve one unified shell across home, batch, and PSD workflow pages; let chat be the only page that feels more conversational.
4. Make primary actions obvious and singular; secondary actions should stay visually subordinate.
5. Use stable, desktop-first, low-surprise interactions with clear loading, empty, and error states.
